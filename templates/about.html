About the Party

History, Core Values, and Principles

Our History

The Origins of Nagkakaisang Tugon

The organization was founded by Former Professor Felix David, whose vision was to create a community that encourages students to develop a deeper understanding of social realities while strengthening their moral and spiritual values. Through his leadership and guidance, the organization began its journey as a platform for students who wished to actively engage with societal issues in a meaningful and responsible way.

Core Values

Our Guiding Principles

Nagbabago (Transformation)

The principle of Nagbabago emphasizes the importance of transformation through a deeper understanding of societal issues and personal experiences. When individuals take the time to reflect and analyze the problems surrounding them, they gain new perspectives that may influence their attitudes, beliefs, and actions.

Through awareness and education, members are encouraged to grow and transform themselves so they can respond to challenges with wisdom and empathy.

Nagwawasto (Correction)

Nagwawasto highlights the responsibility to correct what is unjust, unfair, or morally wrong. Members are encouraged to recognize and address issues that affect individuals or communities, including inequality, injustice, and harmful practices.

This principle promotes accountability, critical thinking, and the courage to stand for what is right while working toward positive change.

Lumalaban (Struggle for Justice)

The value of Lumalaban reflects the commitment to actively seek solutions to problems and injustices. It represents the collective effort to confront challenges and advocate for meaningful reforms.

However, this struggle is rooted in thoughtful action and responsible engagement, focusing on initiatives and solutions that lead to constructive and lasting results.

Principles

Our Foundation

Active Non-Violence

The organization upholds the principle of Active Non-Violence, which means members remain actively engaged in addressing issues and concerns while rejecting violence as a means of expression or protest.

Instead of resorting to aggression or hostility, members are encouraged to communicate their grievances through peaceful dialogue, constructive discussions, and respectful engagement. This approach ensures that advocacy and action are grounded in understanding, cooperation, and mutual respect.

Authentic Christian Humanism

Authentic Christian Humanism recognizes the inherent dignity and potential within every individual.

  • In every person, there is potential.
    The organization believes that each individual possesses unique talents and capabilities that can contribute to the betterment of society.
  • Everyone is welcome regardless of religion.
    While inspired by Christian values, the organization remains inclusive and open to people of different beliefs and backgrounds.
  • Through God, we develop and utilize our potential.
    Guided by faith, members are encouraged to cultivate their abilities and use them in service to others and the wider community.
