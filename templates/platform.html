General Plan of Action

The Engineering Student Council general plan of action combines seminars, student support systems, competitions, outreach, year-long programs, and tangible campus improvements designed for engineering students.

Platform Overview

Programs built around student growth, welfare, and engagement

This GPOA is organized to support engineering students academically, socially, professionally, and personally through concrete and visible initiatives across the academic year.

Seminars

Leadership, thesis support, AI literacy, calculator mastery, and academic enrichment programs.

Competitive Events

Organization pageants, themed campus events, and sports and esports tournaments that build camaraderie.

Outreach and Welfare

Animal welfare, holiday giving, student concerns channels, and mental health support systems.

Tangible Improvements

Facility inspections, lockers, seating spaces, parking policy improvements, and hygiene access projects.

Seminars

Professional, academic, and leadership development

WomENgineers: Breaking Stigmas

A leadership and professional development seminar centered on women inclusivity, diversity, and empowerment in engineering, with guest speakers and a panel discussion.

ACADEMSic ENlightenment

Voluntary online study sessions and study groups for minor subjects across all year levels, hosted through Google Meet by year representatives or student volunteers, with recordings available for review and reinforcement.

AIgnite: The Future of Artificial Intelligence

A seminar on the responsible and effective use of Artificial Intelligence in learning and engineering work, emphasizing productivity, ethical limits, and the lasting role of human creativity and critical thinking.

LikhaEN: Mastering Your Thesis Journey

A hands-on seminar guiding students through topic selection, research methodology, data analysis, and presentation techniques so they can approach thesis writing with clarity and academic rigor.

COEmpute with Ease: Calculator Techniques Seminar

A practical seminar on advanced calculator techniques for engineering and scientific applications, helping students improve speed, accuracy, and confidence in solving academic problems.

ENSC General Assembly

A welcoming and community-building event, especially for freshmen, featuring games, prizes, introductions to college administrators, and exposure to RSOs within the engineering community.

Competitive Events

Campus energy, talent, and friendly competition

ENKabogable: Built Different

A competition among RSOs with recognition categories such as formal attire, organization shirt, themed presentation, popularity award, runners-up, and Mr. and Ms. ENKabogable, with promotional videos and Pageant Vote PH-based public voting.

ENScares

A Halloween-themed escape room where registered groups face puzzles, problem-solving challenges, and jump scares, with advance scheduling and walk-in slots when reserved teams do not arrive on time.

ENtramurals: Sports and Esports Tournament

A tournament that brings together RSOs through esports such as Mobile Legends, Valorant, Tekken 8, and Call of Duty: Mobile, alongside sports including basketball, volleyball, badminton, chess, dama, darts, and scrabble, subject to alignment with UElympics 2027.

Outreach

Compassion and community-centered service

ENPaws: Feed and Care

In partnership with Youth for Animals, this outreach supports the feeding and care of resident cats at the University of the East while promoting humane treatment and organized campus compassion.

ENReach

A holiday outreach program focused on providing children with gifts, toys, and other goods, continuing a tradition of care for underprivileged communities.

Initiatives

Daily systems that improve the student experience

CENGkonek

A semester-end interactive booth featuring kamustahan sessions where students can openly share experiences, concerns, feedback, and suggestions.

OpEN Line

An anonymous drop box for concerns, feedback, and suggestions about the council, college, and university, with monthly monitoring to ensure matters are addressed.

ENlocked

A council-led project that provides additional secure and accessible lockers to reduce student concerns about safeguarding personal belongings.

ENsights Corner

A start-of-year welcome booth that helps engineering students ask questions, seek assistance, and understand council programs and services from the beginning of the year.

CENGspection

End-of-semester checklist-based inspections of engineering facilities, coordinated with maintenance teams and paired with student reports and progress updates.

ENPARK

An initiative to improve the parking system through a clearer sticker policy, lower rates for motorcycles, higher allocation for motorcycle users, and transparent first-come, first-served distribution.

CENGtuary

An initiative to improve comfort and productivity by placing benches, chairs, and tables across the Engineering building and open spaces so students have accessible areas to study, work, collaborate, and rest during free periods.

Year-Long Events

Programs sustained across the academic year

ENrinig Ka

A monthly online form that lets students submit concerns, suggestions, and feedback to the council, administration, or facilities, followed by endorsements, action tracking, and transparency updates.

ENsync

A semester-based committee member rotation system with open applications for new and returning students, bringing fresh ideas while allowing flexibility around personal commitments.

CENGspiration

A recognition initiative that features exemplary engineering students, competition winners, and student leaders on the official Facebook page as role models for the college community.

ENSCareer Uplifters

A cross-disciplinary series of technical events in partnership with RSOs that helps students produce portfolio-grade outputs aligned with industry hiring standards across BSIT, BSCS, BSCpE, BSECE, BSEE, BSME, and BSCE.

NaENtindihan Ka

A year-long safe-space initiative with designated boxes where students can share feelings anonymously or request support connections with the Guidance Office or Student Affairs Office, supported by mental health infographics and hotline numbers.

ACADEMS

Academic Assistance through Distribution of Course Transcriptions is a student-led review support program that distributes organized transcriptions for Engineering, Mathematics, and Science courses every prelim, midterm, and final period.

Advocacies

Transparency, wellness, and job-readiness

ENvision Wellness Week

A week-long October Mental Health Awareness Month program with seminars, workshops, film showings, and wellness activities that encourage self-care, reflection, and stigma reduction.

TransparENcy

An online financial transparency initiative that shows budget allocation, expenses, savings or remaining balance, and sponsorship details so engineering students can clearly understand how funds are used.

EN-Gauge: Competency Mapping

A yearly skills roadmap and self-diagnostic system that helps students compare their actual skills with industry expectations and identify which technical and soft skills they should strengthen.

Extra-Curricular

Stronger bonds across courses and year levels

ENgageering

A team-building event that groups engineering students from different courses and year levels into mixed teams for collaborative challenges, helping strengthen unity, teamwork, and new connections.

Tangible Project

Direct, physical improvements for student convenience

PROJECT VENdoExtend

A project that improves access to essential hygiene products by adding vending machines on both sides of the third floor stocked with sanitary napkins, wipes, and tissues.
